abstract: "We investigate whether slow market dynamics stem from intrinsic persistence or external drivers. Analyzing S&P 500 correlation matrices using a VIX-coupled model, we find that conditioning on the observed VIX proxy absorbs most of the apparent slow dynamics, reducing relaxation time from 298 to 61 trading days. Multiple robustness checks support the conclusion that volatility conditioning explains the observed persistence in market correlation patterns."
