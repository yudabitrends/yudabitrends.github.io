abstract: "We present a theoretical framework reconceptualizing redundancy as a fundamental aspect of information structure rather than mere inefficiency. We show that redundancy has both upper and lower bounds, creating an optimal equilibrium. While traditional communication theory minimizes redundancy for efficiency, finite structured systems achieve peak stability and generalization performance near this equilibrium point. Masked autoencoder experiments demonstrate that generalization peaks at a stable redundancy level."
